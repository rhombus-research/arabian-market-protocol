# Experimental Findings and Results Reference

This document summarizes the experimental findings from the AMP simulation
study and maps each result to the reproducible artifact that produces it. All
data is regenerated by the corresponding `run_*.py` scripts at the project
root; every table below cites its source JSON in `out/`.

---

## Key findings

| # | Finding | Type |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Penalty ratio alone cannot marginalize a cryptojacker; budget decay is required | Result |
| 2 | The stability boundary at rate=3 is the decay-effectiveness boundary; the flat-mint boundary lies between rate 0 and 1 | Result |
| 3 | The analytical stability inequality is loose by ~5x relative to the empirical boundary | Result |
| 4 | Under fork bomb, CFS gives the critical process 32 dispatches, a well-configured cgroup 21, and a misconfigured cgroup 7 | Baseline result |
| 5 | AMP enforces an implicit budget-rate ceiling at MINT_RATE_ACTIVE_MS on all processes | Limitation |
| 6 | THROTTLED is effectively terminal under decay — no organic recovery, even when demand falls | Limitation |
| 7 | A pulse attacker (30% duty) extracts 20x more dispatches than a constant cryptojacker; AMP never throttles it | Open vulnerability |
| 8 | Heterogeneous workloads starve the lowest-bid processes (demand=1 receives 0 dispatches) | Limitation |
| 9 | 72% idle CPU under cryptojacking-with-decay is specific to the 3-process scenario; a heterogeneous workload recovers to 100% utilization | Result |

Findings 5, 6, 7, and 8 are disclosed as honest limitations and open
vulnerabilities rather than closed defenses.

## Figures

### Two-Boundary Stability Analysis

Two lines on the same plot, x-axis = `mint_rate_throttled` (0..5),
y-axis = critical_dispatch_ratio:

- Flat-mint line: drops from 0.718 → 0.200 between rate=0 and rate=1
- Decay-on line: holds at 0.718 through rate=2, drops to 0.200 at rate=3

Annotations:
- Analytical bound: rate=5
- Empirical flat-mint boundary: rate=1
- 5x gap between analytical and empirical

Source: `out/stability_boundaries.json`

### Heterogeneous Workload Dispatch Distribution

Bar chart, x-axis = pid (1, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 100), y-axis = dispatch
count. Color by final state (ACTIVE green, THROTTLED yellow). Shows the
non-monotonic relationship between demand and dispatches (d1=0, d2=801,
d3=681, d4=85, d5=25, attacker=8).

Source: `out/heterogeneous_summary.json`

### Fork Bomb Comparison

Four-way comparison across RR, CFS, Cgroup (well-config), and AMP
(fee=5), plotting max process count against critical-process dispatches.

Source: `out/baseline_comparison.json`

### Mint Sweep with Baselines

AMP critical_dispatch_ratio across the throttled mint-rate sweep, with
horizontal reference lines for CFS (0.187) and Cgroup well-config
(0.187). The AMP line shows the effectiveness boundary at rate=3.
